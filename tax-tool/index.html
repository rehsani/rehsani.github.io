How much tax would you pay, by county?

Set your income, spending, home value and filing status, and see the total annual tax you would pay in every US county, live. It combines federal (income, Social Security, Medicare), state and local income, sales, and property tax.

Not tax advice. A rough estimate for education only, not tax, legal or financial advice; check your own situation with a qualified professional. Data current as of July 2026 (2025 tax-year rules).

Assumptions & data sources
  • Displayed at county resolution. Sales & local-income taxes (ZIP-level underneath) are population-weighted to a county rate; property tax is county-level; state income tax is state-level. Within-county variation is averaged out.
  • Income type. "Wages" incurs Social Security + Medicare payroll tax; "Retirement / investment" does not (but income tax still applies, and retirement-income exclusions and preferential capital-gains rates are not modeled). Married-jointly uses a single Social Security wage base, so a two-earner couple's payroll tax is slightly understated. No itemized deductions, tax credits (e.g. EITC — overstates very low incomes), or exemption phase-outs; NY's tax-benefit recapture (understates high NY earners) and federal-tax deductibility in AL/LA/MO (overstates those) are also not modeled.
  • Local income tax is modeled in MD, IN, OH, PA, KY, MI, NY, IA, plus Kansas City/St. Louis, Wilmington, and major Alabama cities. Not modeled: Oregon's Portland-metro/Multnomah taxes (they apply only above ~$125k) and minor municipal occupational taxes. Approximations: NYC is a flat 3.5% (actually graduated); Iowa surtaxes use a county average; OH/PA/KY use each ZIP's dominant municipality (small enclaves inherit the larger city's rate); the county value is a population-weighted blend, so a partly-taxed county shows a partial rate.
  • Sales tax applies to the taxable share of spending you set (default 65%); rent and many services/groceries are exempt in reality. Combined rates use the lowest rate within each 5-digit ZIP where a ZIP spans jurisdictions.
  • Property tax = county median effective rate × the house price you enter (ignores assessment caps/homestead exemptions, e.g. California Prop 13). Connecticut's rates are its 2023 planning-region values mapped onto the legacy counties. A few counties with suppressed Census data show as no-data (gray).
  • Color scale spans the true minimum-to-maximum total tax for your current inputs, and re-scales whenever you change an input — so shading is relative to the current scenario, not an absolute dollar scale, and two scenarios aren't directly color-comparable.
  • Sources: Census ACS 5-yr 2023 (property), Streamlined Sales Tax + state DORs (sales), state DORs (income), municipal/county registers (local income). Rates reflect 2025 law; a few source files are effective into 2026 (e.g. some sales-tax tables). Not tax advice — a directional estimate.
